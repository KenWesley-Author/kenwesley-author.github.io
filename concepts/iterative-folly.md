"A person repeats his mistake twice, he can safely be labelled a fool. He goes on to commit stupidity for the seventh, the tenth time. By the eleventh, he succeeds, he is vindicated."

---


Concept

Repeated error that only gains legitimacy through eventual success.


---


Explanation

A sequence of failed actions is judged in isolation as stupidity.
Repetition reinforces this judgment.

Yet the sequence is not complete.

When success finally occurs, the entire chain is reinterpreted:

What was once called folly becomes persistence

What was once stupidity becomes determination

What was once error becomes process

Judgment was premature.
Outcome rewrites meaning.
