People tend to double down on their mistakes, until they are defeated utterly and totally.

Concept

Escalation of Error
The compulsion to reinforce a mistake through continued commitment, ending in total defeat.

Explanation

A mistake is rarely abandoned at its origin.

Instead:

It is defended
Repeated
Reinforced

Each attempt to correct it deepens it.

What begins as error becomes commitment.
What becomes commitment resists correction.

The process continues—until reality intervenes.

Structure
Initial Error → recognized but not corrected
Reinforcement → repeated justification and action
Escalation → increasing investment in the mistake
Collapse → total and undeniable defeat
Insight

A mistake defended becomes a commitment; a commitment defended becomes ruin.

Implication
Error persists not from ignorance, but from refusal to withdraw
The cost of correction increases with time
Most failures end not in correction, but in exhaustion or collapse
Related Concepts
Iterative Folly
Sunk-Cost Spiral
